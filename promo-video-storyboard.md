# Life Words Promo Video — Storyboard & Script

**Total runtime:** 4:00–4:30
**Tone:** Warm, hopeful, unhurried. Speak clearly and slightly slower than conversational pace — the audience includes people with cognitive impairments and their families.
**Music:** Gentle acoustic guitar or soft piano underscore throughout. Uplifting but never urgent.

---

## ACT 1 — THE PROBLEM (0:00–0:45)

### SHOT 1 — Cold Open (0:00–0:12)
**Visual:** Slow fade in. Close-up of an older person sitting at a kitchen table with family photos on the wall behind them. They look at a photo of a woman (their daughter) on the table. Their expression is warm but frustrated — they open their mouth to say something, pause, and look down.
**Audio:** Ambient room tone. No music yet.
**Voiceover:** *"You know her face. You know her laugh. But the name just... won't come."*

### SHOT 2 — The Challenge (0:12–0:30)
**Visual:** Slow montage of everyday moments — a person struggling to recall a word at dinner, someone hesitating during a phone call, a hand reaching for a family photo album. Soft focus, warm lighting.
**Audio:** Soft piano begins, single sustained notes.
**Voiceover:** *"For the 2 million Americans living with aphasia — and millions more with brain injuries, dementia, or neurological conditions — the words that connect us to the people we love can slip away."*

### SHOT 3 — The Turning Point (0:30–0:45)
**Visual:** Same person from Shot 1 now picks up their phone or tablet. The screen glows. Cut to a close-up of the Life Words landing page: "Rebuild Your Words, Reclaim Your Life." They tap "Start Practice."
**Audio:** Music lifts slightly — a second instrument joins.
**Voiceover:** *"Life Words was built to help you get them back."*

## ACT 2 — THE PRODUCT WALKTHROUGH (0:45–3:15)

### SHOT 4 — The Dashboard (0:45–1:05)
**Visual:** Screen recording or recreation of the dashboard. Show the welcome card: "Welcome back, Margaret!" with the blue "Start Now" button. The cursor/finger taps "Start Now."
**Camera direction:** Start slightly zoomed out to show the full page, then slowly push in on the "My Life Words and Memory" card.
**Voiceover:** *"When you open Life Words, you're greeted by name. Your dashboard is simple — one clear path to practice. No clutter, no confusion."*

### SHOT 5 — Adding Contacts (1:05–1:40)
**Visual:** The "Manage My People" page. Show 3 contact cards already added — one with a photo of a woman labeled "Sarah, Daughter," one with a dog labeled "Max," and one amber-bordered card for "Dr. Chen" marked "Needs photo."
**Action sequence:**
- (1:05–1:15) Camera pans across the existing contact cards
- (1:15–1:25) Finger taps "+ Add with Details" — the form appears. Show someone typing a name, selecting "Daughter" as relationship, and uploading a photo from their camera roll
- (1:25–1:40) The new card appears in the grid. Cut to a close-up of the completed card with the photo.
**Voiceover:** *"You start by adding the people and things in your life. Upload a photo of your daughter. Your dog. Your doctor. These aren't stock images from a textbook — they're your photos, your life, your words."*

### SHOT 6 — Choosing Practice Type (1:40–2:00)
**Visual:** The Practice Hub page. Show the three large practice buttons stacked vertically:
- "Name Practice" (blue)
- "Question Practice" (purple)
- "Information Practice" (green/teal)

Below them, the icon grid: Quick Add, Instructions, Progress, Messages, My People, My Info, My Stuff, Settings.
**Action:** Finger hovers over each practice type briefly, then taps "Name Practice."
**Voiceover:** *"Choose how you want to practice. Name Practice asks you to say someone's name from their photo. Question Practice asks things like 'What does Sarah do for work?' And Information Practice helps you recall personal details. Each mode builds different language skills."*

### SHOT 7 — A Practice Session (2:00–2:50) ★ STAR SEQUENCE
**Visual:** This is the emotional core of the video. Show a full Name Practice session with 3 phases for one contact.

**Phase A — The Prompt (2:00–2:10)**
Screen shows Sarah's photo, large and centered. Below it: "Your daughter." At the bottom, a blue microphone button with "Tap to speak."
**Voiceover:** *"A photo appears — someone you know and love. Take your time."*

**Phase B — Listening (2:10–2:25)**
Finger taps the microphone. The button turns green and pulses. "Listening..." appears below. Cut to a close-up of the person's face — they concentrate, then say: "Sarah."
**Audio:** The word "Sarah" spoken naturally, slightly hesitant, then confident. Background music dips to near-silence during the speech.
**Voiceover:** *(Pause during the speech moment — let the audience hear the person speak)*

**Phase C — Success (2:25–2:50)**
The screen shows a green checkmark. 'You said: "Sarah"' appears with "Correct!" below it. Cut to the person smiling.
**Action:** Show one more round — the next contact photo appears (Max the dog). The person taps the mic and says "Max." Another green checkmark.
Show the "Get Hint" button being used on a third attempt — a text hint appears: "Starts with D..." and the person says "Dr. Chen." Correct again.
**Audio:** A soft, satisfying chime on each correct answer. Music swells gently.
**Voiceover:** *"The app listens, understands, and tells you right away. And if you need a little help — hints are always there. No judgment, just encouragement."*

### SHOT 8 — Progress Tracking (2:50–3:15)
**Visual:** The Progress page. Show the three stat cards at the top:
- Total Sessions: 24
- Activities Tried: 3
- Encouragement message

Then scroll down to "Progress by Activity" — show accuracy percentages climbing. Highlight a row showing "85% accuracy" in Name Practice.
**Action:** Animate or highlight the stats appearing, as if the numbers are counting up.
**Voiceover:** *"Every session is tracked. You'll see your accuracy improve, your response times get faster, and your confidence grow. Share your progress with your speech therapist to keep your treatment on track."*

**Visual:** The pricing page. Show both plan cards:

Then transition to the landing page hero: "Rebuild Your Words, Reclaim Your Life" with the "Start Practice" button glowing or pulsing subtly.
**Text overlay (large, centered):** "7-day free trial — no credit card required"

### SHOT 13 — Closing (4:15–4:30)
**Visual:** Return to the person from Shot 1. They're now on a video call with their daughter. They smile and say "Hi, Sarah" naturally and confidently. The daughter smiles back. Slow fade to the Life Words logo on a clean white background.
**Text overlay:** `words.parrotsoftware.com`
**Audio:** Music resolves to a warm final chord. Silence.
**Voiceover:** *"Life Words. Your words. Your life. Your way back."*

### Voiceover Direction
- **Voice:** Warm, mid-range. Male or female. Think "trusted friend," not "infomercial host."
- **Pace:** ~130 words per minute (slower than typical VO). Leave breathing room between sentences.
- **Emphasis:** Lean into personal words — "your photos," "your daughter," "your life."
- **Shot 7B pause:** Do NOT voice over the moment the person says "Sarah." Let the audience hear it. This is the emotional peak.
